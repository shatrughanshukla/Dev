Breaking News: Major Event Unfolds

By Jane Smith August 10, 2024

Group Captain Shubhanshu Shukla made history as the first Indian to dock with the International Space Station on June 26, 2025, aboard Axiom Mission 4 via SpaceX’s Dragon. His mission, conducted in partnership with ISRO, NASA, and DBT, sparked national pride and emotional celebrations across India.

On June 26, 2025, Group Captain Shubhanshu Shukla became the first Indian to dock with the International Space Station (ISS), marking a monumental milestone in India’s space journey. He was part of Axiom Mission 4 (Ax-4), which launched aboard SpaceX’s Dragon spacecraft from NASA’s Kennedy Space Center on June 25. The mission is a collaborative effort involving NASA, Axiom Space, and ISRO, with the goal of conducting microgravity experiments developed by Indian researchers. Shukla will spend approximately two weeks aboard the ISS, working on scientific studies supported by the Department of Biotechnology (DBT) and ISRO. His journey echoes the legacy of Wing Commander Rakesh Sharma, the first Indian in space in 1984. The successful launch and docking have ignited nationwide pride, with emotional reactions from his hometown of Lucknow and congratulations pouring in from Indian officials, ISRO scientists, and international space agencies. Shukla’s mission is expected to significantly contribute to India's growing role in global space exploration and deepen international cooperation in science and technology.
